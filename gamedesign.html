Central Processing

Central Processing is a cooperative card-based puzzle game for two players about learning elements of binary logic â€” the building blocks of computer code. We wanted to make the game as freely accessible as possible: it can be played with nothing more than the rules, a pencil and paper, and a standard deck of playing cards. Or, you can print and play our custom deck.

See the gameplay instructions here, or the version with an online randomizer on my groupmate's website here.

Created by Luca Guerrera, Ivy Huang, and Ford McDill. I collaborated with Ivy and Ford in designing the game and iterative playtesting of the rules. I also designed the custom deck from scratch.
